## PART 1: THE CRISIS OF VIBE CODING (continued)

### 1.2 ANATOMY OF FAILURE

The anatomy of vibe coding unfolds as a meticulous dissection of a system where four key defects systematically undermine the project, creating the illusion of activity amid a complete absence of sustainable progress. The first defect, known as contextual drift, manifests in the systematic loss of architectural integrity after a limited number of interactions with the model. During several consecutive prompts, the AI loses information about previously made decisions, data types, and organizational principles, leading to module inconsistencies and the disintegration of the overall structure into disconnected fragments. This isolation mechanism for each request turns development into a sequence of disparate experiments where accumulated experience is not passed on, exacerbating chaos at every stage.[mcp:1]

The second defect, implementation-level hallucinations, involves the generation of non-existent constructs that the model presents with full confidence in their legitimacy. Examples include references to fictional libraries or API methods absent from real specifications, causing immediate runtime failures. These artifacts arise from the nature of language models oriented toward probabilistic sequence prediction rather than verified facts, leading to chain reactions of errors that require significant human effort for localization and elimination. As a result, code that appears elegant at the generation stage proves unfit for production environments without deep human intervention.[mcp:1]

The third defect, endless fix loops, forms a vicious cycle in which fixing one problem inevitably provokes the emergence of new ones, often related to the previous. The refactoring process for one element revives previously closed vulnerabilities in altered form, hindering any linear advancement and accumulating technical debt in geometric progression. The absence of cumulative learning mechanisms in the model amplifies this effect, turning each iteration into repetitive labor without guaranteed quality improvement. Ultimately, the time spent on such cycles far exceeds the costs of traditional approaches, undermining the primary goal of using AI.[mcp:1]

The fourth defect, scalability failure, becomes evident when attempting to apply the approach to real-complexity projects beyond demonstration prototypes. Simple structures with minimal operations are mastered successfully by the model, but introducing parallelism, distributed components, or security requirements causes complete system collapse. Industrial precedents, such as the Replit data incident or mass failures of AI-generated repositories on GitHub, confirm this pattern, where 70 percent of such code carries critical vulnerabilities according to independent audits. The lack of built-in safeguards leads to exponential risk growth as volume and load increase.[mcp:1]

The complex of these defects is rooted in the fundamental limitations of models lacking long-term memory and self-regulation mechanisms, making vibe coding vulnerable to illusions of 'intuitive' creativity. The only effective countermeasure is the introduction of strict protocols akin to Whip Coding, with context separation, multi-level verification, and fixed task boundaries. This approach transforms the chaotic environment into a manageable process, ensuring a transition from problem diagnosis to their constructive resolution.[mcp:1]
